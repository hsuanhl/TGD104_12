About

Introduction

The Museum of Visionary Art is a unique institution dedicated to showcasing the work of artists who work outside of the traditional art world. The museum was founded in the early 2000s by a group of art collectors and enthusiasts who were fascinated by the work of self-taught and outsider artists.

The museum's collection includes a wide variety of media, from paintings and sculptures to installation and performance art. Many of the artists featured in the collection work in a visionary or surrealist style, creating fantastical worlds and otherworldly imagery.

Collection

One of the most popular exhibits at the Museum of Visionary Art is the annual "Fringe Artists" show, which highlights the work of emerging outsider artists from around the world. The show is known for its eclectic mix of styles and mediums, and is always a hit with visitors.

In addition to its exhibitions, the museum also hosts a variety of educational programs, workshops, and events. These programs are designed to introduce visitors to the world of visionary art and provide them with a deeper understanding of the artists and their work.

Building

The museum's location in a renovated industrial building in the heart of the city also adds to its unique charm. The building's history is well preserved, with many of its original features still in place, including exposed brick walls, steel beams and large windows.

The Museum of Visionary Art is not just a place for art lovers, but for anyone interested in expanding their perspective and experiencing something new. It's a must-see destination for anyone visiting the city and a unique and inspiring experience for anyone looking to explore the world of visionary art.
